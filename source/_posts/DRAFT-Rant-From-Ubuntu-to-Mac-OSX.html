Warning, this is a rant!
Mac is ok, but not great.

- Command+Q is annoying, things stay open otherwise! (ctrl+d in terminal, :q in vim)
- Xcode, Mac developer account, etc. was painful!
- Home and End buttons don't always work! Use command+arrows, but sometimes that has another function like in Adium to switchtabs, or back and forward in a browser.
- iTerm2 is featureful, reminds me on Konsole (splits), integrates with Tmux!
- The terminal is comfortable, but some commands are different (BSD vs GNU)
- Spaces & Mission control is pretty good
- Wheel click defaults to the dashboard, can't open links in tabs in firefox, fixed in settings
- F keys default to secondary behaviour, I changed them back in the settings (F3 in Firefox)
- Cant just click on the terminal in the dock to open a new (additional) one
- I can never remember how to screenshot! F1[3-5] seem to have replaced those keys :/

apt-get == brew (but slower because it compiles from source)
libnotify == Growl (costs money in Lion)
Compiz grid, etc. == Cinch, Divy, etc. (none of these are free!)
A system monitor widget in the dock even costs money!
pidgin == Adium (both based on libpurple)
gvim == mvim (no build ctags button)

I've gained the ability to run MS Office for Mac seamlessly and OSX familiarity.
Dual screen support is really great!
Power and USB ports in the screen is nice.
Magnetic power plug :/ yipee
